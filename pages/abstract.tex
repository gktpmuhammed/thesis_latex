\chapter{\abstractname}

This thesis investigates organ-aware radiology report generation from 3D chest CT using vision-language foundation models. The main challenge is to preserve clinically relevant anatomical information while keeping the visual token interface compact and stable for language decoding.

We propose an end-to-end pipeline that combines volumetric CT preprocessing with organ-mask supervision, LLM-based decomposition of reports into organ-level targets, organ-conditioned visual token extraction from a 3D vision encoder, and decoder adaptation with parameter-efficient fine-tuning. The framework is designed for protocol-matched comparison across MedGemma, GPT-2, BioBART, and a fVLM-aligned BERT-base baseline.

Experiments on CT-RATE show that interface design has higher impact than architectural complexity. In controlled ablations, the strongest MedGemma variant improves major global quality and clinical-consistency signals over the baseline. In decoder-family comparison under the same evaluation protocol, the finalized rows show a mixed ranking: the fVLM-aligned BERT-base baseline is strongest on overall GREEN and broad lexical-semantic quality, while MedGemma remains strongest on relational clinical structure by RadGraph. Additional diversity diagnostics show higher template reuse in BERT outputs, whereas MedGemma outputs are less repetitive. GPT-2 ranks above BioBART on most global dimensions.

Overall, the thesis provides a reproducible organ-aware 3D report generation framework, code-grounded ablation evidence, and a complete protocol-matched cross-decoder comparison.
